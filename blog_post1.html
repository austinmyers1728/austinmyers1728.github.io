ornstein

I'm not sure what the purpose of this blog might end up being, nor if it will be used whatsoever, but I thought it might as well be a nice touch to include on a website. So, as an inaugural blog post, maybe just to get the space filled, I thought I would share some interesting music I became aware of a few months ago. Leo Ornstein was perhaps one of the more well-known/prolific American composers of the early 20th century, and in fact was often somewhat ahead of the curve in terms of the mainstream European avant-garde. As far as I knew from music history, Ornstein soon after fell off the radar into obscurity and seemingly nothing of note was produced post ~1920. Below is a performance of his fairly well-known 4th Piano Sonata from 1918:

To my surprise, however, despite remaining outside of the public eye, Ornstein continued to compose a large body of music until his (fairly recent) death in 2002. His later music lacks much of the shock value his most famous works are known for, but I think this resulted in much more pleasant compositions which still have some nice subtleties to offer.
